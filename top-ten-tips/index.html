    Top Ten Tips

    Wisdom from Experience

    Essential advice for working in the Emergency Department - simple principles that make a big difference.

    1. Respect Those Around You

    Value Every Team Member

    Respect those around you and value their opinion.

    • Nurses often know patients better than you
    • Allied health professionals are experts in their field
    • Patients know their own bodies
    • Your colleagues are there to help you

    2. The History Is Everything

    Listen First

    The history is everything.

    Most diagnoses are made from the history alone. Examination and investigations confirm what you already suspect. Take the time to listen properly.

    3. Four Key Treatments

    Think About These Four Things for Every Patient

    There are 4 key treatments we give in the ED – think whether every patient you see needs any of these and you will save lives and relieve pain:

    1. Oxygen - Is the patient hypoxic?
    2. Fluids - Are they dehydrated or shocked?
    3. Analgesia - Are they in pain?
    4. Antibiotics - Do they have sepsis?

    4. Make a Difference

    Every Patient Matters

    Think "What difference have I made to this patient?"

    Always try to make a difference, however small. It may "just" be getting them an extra blanket, but it matters to them.

    5. Remember They Don't Want to Be Here

    Nobody Plans to Come to ED

    No patient (almost) wants to be in the ED. They really don't.

    It wasn't what they planned for their day. Be kind, be patient, and understand their frustration.

    6. Double Your Time for Difficult Interactions

    When It's Hard, Try Harder

    Spend twice as long with patients you don't like or don't get on with.

    • There's usually a reason for difficult behavior
    • Taking extra time often resolves conflict
    • You learn the most from challenging interactions
    • They need your help just as much as anyone else

    7. Look the Part

    Professional Appearance Matters

    Look the part. Be smart. Behave in the way you would expect anyone to behave towards you.

    • Patients judge your competence by your appearance
    • Professional behavior builds trust
    • Set the standard you expect from others

    8. Be On Time

    Punctuality Is Respect

    Be on time. Always. And leave on time if at all possible.

    • Your colleagues are relying on you
    • Being late affects patient care
    • Leaving on time protects your wellbeing
    • Handover properly so you can leave guilt-free

    9. Take Your Breaks

    Self-Care Isn't Selfish

    Take your breaks – eat when you can and drink water when you can't.

    • You can't look after patients if you don't look after yourself
    • Dehydration and hunger affect your decision-making
    • Regular breaks prevent burnout
    • Keep a water bottle with you

    10. Enjoy Yourself

    Find the Joy

    Enjoy yourself…

    Emergency Medicine can be incredibly rewarding. Find moments of satisfaction in:

    • Making a difficult diagnosis
    • Relieving someone's pain
    • Learning something new
    • Working with a great team
    • Making a real difference to someone's life

    If you're not finding any joy, talk to someone about it.

    The Most Important Thing

    Remember

    Every patient is someone's loved one. Treat them as you would want your own family treated.
