    Clinical Assessment

    Red Flags

    • Loss of consciousness >5 minutes
    • Post-traumatic amnesia
    • Repeated vomiting
    • Focal neurological deficit
    • Skull fracture
    • Seizure
    • Dangerous mechanism of injury
    • GCS <15 at 2 hours post-injury
    • Coagulopathy or anticoagulation

    CT Head Indications

    NICE recommendations for CT head imaging in adults with head injury:

    Immediate CT (within 1 hour)

    • GCS <13 on initial assessment
    • GCS <15 at 2 hours post-injury
    • Suspected open or depressed skull fracture
    • Sign of basal skull fracture (haemotympanum, 'panda' eyes, CSF leak, Battle's sign)
    • Post-traumatic seizure
    • Focal neurological deficit
    • More than one episode of vomiting

    CT within 8 hours

    • Loss of consciousness or amnesia AND any of:
      • Age 65 years
      • Dangerous mechanism (pedestrian struck by vehicle, occupant ejected from vehicle, fall from height >1 meter)
      • Coagulopathy (history of bleeding disorder, on anticoagulation)

    Anticoagulation

    Patients on anticoagulation (warfarin, DOACs) with even minor head injury should have low threshold for CT imaging.
