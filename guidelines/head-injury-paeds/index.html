    Paediatric Head Injury

    Quick Reference

    CT head rules and management guidance for paediatric head injuries.

    PREDICT Algorithm

    The PREDICT (Paediatric Research in Emergency Departments International Collaborative) algorithm helps determine which children require CT imaging after head injury.

    Open PDF in new tab

    Key Points

    • PREDICT is validated for children aged 0-18 years
    • Helps reduce unnecessary CT scans
    • Based on large international cohort study
    • Use in conjunction with clinical judgment

    High-Risk Features

    Immediate CT Required

    Any of the following warrant urgent CT head:

    • GCS <14 (or GCS <15 if <1 year old)
    • Focal neurological deficit
    • Signs of skull fracture or basal skull fracture
    • Altered mental state
    • Persistent vomiting (>3 episodes)
    • Post-traumatic seizure
    • Dangerous mechanism (high-speed motor vehicle accident, fall >1m if <2 years, fall >1.5m if â‰¥2 years)
    • Suspected non-accidental injury

    Observation

    Admission for Observation

    Consider admission for:

    • Any intracranial injury on CT
    • Persistent symptoms despite normal CT
    • Difficulty assessing child (e.g., very young, developmental delay)
    • Concern about caregiver supervision
    • Social concerns

    Discharge Criteria

    Safe to discharge if:

    • Normal neurological examination
    • GCS 15
    • No high-risk features
    • Reliable caregiver
    • Easy access to ED if concerns

    Discharge Information

    Patient Information Sheet

    Provide written concussion advice to all caregivers at discharge.

    Download Starship Concussion Advice Sheet

    Head Injury Advice

    Return immediately if:

    • Increased drowsiness or difficulty waking
    • Vomiting
    • Severe headache
    • Seizure or unusual movements
    • Weakness in arms or legs
    • Vision changes
    • Unusual behaviour or confusion
    • Clear fluid from nose or ears

    Concussion Management

    Return to Activity

    Graduated return to activity after concussion:

    1. Complete rest - 24-48 hours
    2. Light activity - walking, light housework
    3. Moderate activity - jogging, light swimming
    4. Heavy non-contact - running, gym work
    5. Full contact practice
    6. Return to competition

    Progress only if symptom-free at each stage. If symptoms return, drop back to previous level.
