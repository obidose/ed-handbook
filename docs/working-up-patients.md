# :material-stethoscope: Working Up a Patient

!!! info "Ongoing Patient Management"
    After your initial review and plan (with or without senior discussion), some patients will need ongoing workup in the department.

---

## :material-pill: Essential Prescribing

### Medications to Consider

!!! example "Ensure Comfort and Treatment"
    Ensure patients have all medications they will need prescribed:
    
    - **Analgesia** - Don't leave patients in pain
    - **Antiemetics** - For nausea and vomiting
    - **Antibiotics** - If infection suspected or confirmed
    - **Critical regular medications** - Don't miss essential meds
    - **Other symptom control** - As appropriate

!!! warning "Don't Forget Regular Medications"
    - Check medication history
    - Ensure time-critical medications are given (e.g., anticoagulants, anticonvulsants, insulin)
    - Don't assume nursing staff know about regular medications
    - Prescribe them clearly in MedChart

## :material-account-multiple: Managing Multiple Patients

### Parallel Patient Management

!!! tip "You Don't Need to Wait"
    Where there is likely to be a **wait** before a patient is ready for disposition:
    
    - **Pick up the next patient**
    - You do not need to completely finish with one patient before starting on the next
    - Balance multiple patients simultaneously
    - Prioritize based on urgency and needs

### Juggling Priorities

!!! success "Efficient Workflow"
    - Use waiting time productively
    - While patient A waits for CT, see patient B
    - While patient B waits for bloods, review patient A's CT
    - Keep track of all your patients and their status
    - Don't lose track of anyone

## :material-message-alert: Communication

### Keep the Team Informed

!!! warning "Essential Communication"
    - **Stay in touch with your senior** to keep them up to date
    - Make sure the **nursing staff** are kept informed
    - Ensure the **nurse in charge** knows your plans
    - Update the team about any changes

### Why Communication Matters

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

## :material-clock-alert: Time-Limited Plans

### Don't Wait Indefinitely

!!! danger "Always Have a Time Limit"
    **Always have a time-limited plan in mind**
    
    - Where investigation or management is likely to take **more than a couple of hours**:
        - It is probable that they should **not remain under ED care**
        - Talk to your senior if you are not sure
        - Always have a **plan for progression** in mind
    
    - ED is for acute assessment and stabilization, not prolonged workup
    - Consider if patient needs admission for ongoing management

### When to Escalate

!!! warning "Signs You Need to Change Course"
    - Investigations taking longer than expected
    - Patient not improving as anticipated
    - Waiting more than 2-3 hours for next step
    - Becoming unclear about the plan
    - Patient deteriorating

    **Action:** Discuss with your senior - patient may need admission or different approach

---

## :material-update: Reassessment

### Regular Review

!!! success "Active Management"
    - Set specific review times
    - Re-examine patients after key investigations
    - Look for clinical improvement or deterioration
    - Update your plan based on new information

### Be Flexible

!!! tip "Adapt Your Approach"
    - Initial diagnosis may be wrong
    - New symptoms may emerge
    - Investigation results may be unexpected
    - Patient's condition may change
    
    **Don't be afraid to change your plan**

---

## :material-alert-circle: Red Flags During Workup

!!! danger "When to Escalate Immediately"
    - **Clinical deterioration** - Worsening vital signs or consciousness
    - **Unexpected investigation results** - Critically abnormal findings
    - **Diagnostic uncertainty** - Unclear what's going on
    - **Time dragging** - Been working patient up for >2-3 hours
    - **Resource constraints** - Can't get investigations or specialist input needed
    
    **Get senior help early** - don't struggle alone

---

## :material-checkbox-marked-circle: Workup Checklist

!!! tip "During Patient Workup"
    - [ ] Essential medications prescribed (pain, nausea, antibiotics)
    - [ ] Regular medications continued
    - [ ] Investigations ordered and pending list checked
    - [ ] Time-limited plan established
    - [ ] Senior kept informed
    - [ ] Nursing staff aware of plan
    - [ ] Set review time based on investigation results
    - [ ] Other patients picked up while waiting
    - [ ] Patient reassessed after key interventions
    - [ ] Ready to move to disposition (discharge/refer)
