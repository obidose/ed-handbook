# :material-phone-forward: Referring Patients for Admission

!!! info "Making Specialty Referrals"
    Guidelines for referring patients who require admission to inpatient specialties.

---

## :material-clock-check: When to Refer

!!! success "Early Referral"
    - Once it has been decided that a patient **needs admission**, refer them
    - **It is not necessary to await the results of all investigations** before referring
    - Focus on critical tests that affect immediate management

### Investigations Before Referral

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

-   :material-check-circle:{ .lg .middle } __Essential__

    ---

    - Critical investigations that affect **immediate care**
    - Basic workup which will affect disposition (bloods, imaging if urgent)

-   :material-close-circle:{ .lg .middle } __Not Required__

    ---

    - Complete diagnostic workup
    - Definitive treatment investigations
    - Tests that won't change immediate management

</div>

!!! tip "Start Treatment While Waiting"
    - Begin appropriate treatment while awaiting specialty review
    - Don't delay referral for non-critical investigations
    - Specialty can request additional tests after acceptance

---

## :material-phone: How to Refer

### Making Contact

!!! example "Referral Process"
    1. **Identify the appropriate specialty**
    2. **Contact the receiving clinician** for that specialty
    3. **State your request** - "I am referring this patient for admission"
    4. **Present the patient** clearly and concisely

### Key Information to Include

!!! info "ISBAR Format for Referrals"
    
    **Identify**
    
    - Patient name, age, location
    - Your name and role
    
    **Situation**
    
    - Presenting complaint
    - Working diagnosis
    - Clinical urgency
    
    **Background**
    
    - Relevant medical history
    - Current medications
    - Allergies
    
    **Assessment**
    
    - Vital signs and clinical state
    - Key examination findings
    - Investigation results
    
    **Recommendation**
    
    - Why admission is needed
    - What you're asking for (admission, review, take over care)
    - Any time-critical issues

## :material-account-check: Understanding Referral vs Consultation

!!! warning "Referring vs Asking for Advice"
    
    **By default, you are NOT asking for advice - you are REFERRING a patient**
    
    - The specialty to which you refer **must accept** the patient
    - They must **arrange to review** that patient
    - They take over responsibility for that patient's care

### When It's a Consultation (Not Referral)

- You're asking for **specialist input** but maintaining care
- Patient may not need admission
- Seeking advice on investigation or management
- Be clear about what you're requesting

## :material-alert-octagon: Disputed Referrals

### If There Is Disagreement

!!! danger "Escalation Process"
    1. **Remain professional** and document the conversation
    2. **Clarify the disagreement** - Is it about specialty allocation or admission need?
    3. **Talk to your senior early** - Don't try to resolve alone
    4. **Consult the [Allocation of Clinical Line Policy](referrals.md)** for guidance
    5. **Escalate through appropriate channels** if needed

### Common Disputes

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

-   :material-help-circle:{ .lg .middle } __Specialty Allocation__

    ---

    - Unclear which specialty should take the patient
    - Multiple specialties potentially involved
    - **Solution:** Refer to [Allocation of Clinical Line Policy](referrals.md)

-   :material-hospital-box:{ .lg .middle } __Need for Admission__

    ---

    - Specialty questions if admission needed
    - Suggestions for discharge with outpatient follow-up
    - **Solution:** Senior ED doctor involvement early

</div>

!!! tip "Documentation"
    - Document who you spoke to and when
    - Record their response
    - Note any disagreements or conditions
    - Include senior involvement in decision making

---

## :material-file-document-edit: Documentation

### WebPAS Documentation

!!! example "Required Documentation"
    - **Specialty referred to** and clinician spoken to
    - **Time of referral call**
    - **Acceptance** or any conditions
    - **Expected time of review** by specialty
    - **Plan until reviewed**

### Clinical Notes

!!! info "Include in Your Notes"
    - Reason for admission
    - Discussion with specialty team
    - Their acceptance and plan
    - Any interim management required

---

## :material-handshake: Working with Specialties

### Building Good Relationships

!!! success "Professional Communication"
    - Be respectful and clear
    - Have all information ready before calling
    - Know your patient well
    - Accept feedback graciously
    - Learn from each interaction

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid

!!! warning "Don't..."
    - Call without having assessed the patient first
    - Call without basic workup done (but don't wait for non-essential results)
    - Be vague about what you're asking for
    - Argue if you're uncertain - get senior help
    - Take "no" without senior involvement if patient genuinely needs admission

---

## :material-clock-time-four: After Referral

### Ongoing Responsibility

!!! warning "You're Still Responsible"
    Until the specialty has **physically reviewed** and accepted the patient:
    
    - Monitor the patient's condition
    - Escalate any deterioration immediately
    - Continue management as planned
    - Chase up if review delayed

## :material-lightbulb: Quick Tips

!!! tip "Smooth Referrals"
    - **Prepare before calling** - Have all info at hand
    - **Be confident** - You've done the assessment
    - **Have a senior available** if you anticipate issues
    - **Document everything** clearly
    - **Follow up** - Don't assume they'll come
    - **Learn the [Allocation of Clinical Line Policy](referrals.md)** - Saves time and disputes
