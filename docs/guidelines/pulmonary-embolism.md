# :material-lungs: Pulmonary Embolism

## :material-calculator: Clinical Decision Tools

!!! note inline end "PERC Rule"
    Use to exclude PE in low-risk patients without further testing.
    
    [:octicons-link-external-24: Calculator](https://www.mdcalc.com/calc/347/perc-rule-pulmonary-embolism){:target="_blank" .md-button}

!!! success inline end "sPESI Score"
    Identifies low-risk patients suitable for outpatient management.
    
    [:octicons-link-external-24: Calculator](https://www.mdcalc.com/calc/1304/simplified-pesi-pulmonary-embolism-severity-index){:target="_blank" .md-button}

**Pulmonary Embolism Rule-out Criteria (PERC)** helps exclude PE without testing in low-risk patients.

**Simplified Pulmonary Embolism Severity Index (sPESI)** stratifies risk and guides disposition decisions.

## :material-alert: High-Risk PE Features

!!! danger "Urgent Assessment Required"
    - **Haemodynamic instability** (hypotension, shock)
    - **Right ventricular dysfunction** on echo
    - **Elevated troponin or BNP**
    - **Consider thrombolysis** for massive PE

---

## :material-lightbulb: Key Points

!!! tip "Clinical Pearls"
    - **Use validated clinical decision rules** (Wells score, PERC)
    - **D-dimer** has high sensitivity but low specificity
    - **CTPA** is gold standard for diagnosis
    - **Risk stratify** all confirmed PE patients
    - **Consider pregnancy-specific pathway** for pregnant patients
