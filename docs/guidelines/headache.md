!!! tip "Key Clinical Points"
    - Identify red flags requiring urgent investigation
    - Classify headache type (primary vs secondary)
    - Appropriate initial investigations
    - Evidence-based management approaches
    
!!! danger "Red Flags for Headache"
    - Sudden onset ("thunderclap")
    - New onset in >50 years
    - Progressive or changing pattern
    - Neurological deficit
    - Fever or systemic symptoms
    - Postural headache
    - Triggered by cough, sneeze, or Valsalva
    - New headache in immunocompromised or cancer patient
