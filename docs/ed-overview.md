# :material-hospital-box: Emergency Department Overview

!!! info "Welcome to Palmerston North Hospital Emergency Department"
    An overview of our department layout, zones, and key facilities to help you navigate and understand our working environment.

## :material-map: Department Zones

Our Emergency Department is divided into several clinical zones:

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

-   :material-alert:{ .lg .middle } __Resuscitation Area__

    ---

    High-acuity patients requiring immediate intervention and close monitoring.

    - Full monitoring and resuscitation equipment
    - Direct access for ambulance arrivals
    - Critically unwell patients

-   :material-bed:{ .lg .middle } __Acute Assessment Area__

    ---

    Moderate to high-acuity patients requiring assessment and stabilization.

    - Urgent care and treatment
    - Monitoring facilities
    - Close to nursing station

-   :material-account-group:{ .lg .middle } __Sub-Acute Area__

    ---

    Lower-acuity patients with straightforward presentations.

    - Fast-track patients
    - Minor injuries and illnesses
    - Quicker turnover

-   :material-chair-rolling:{ .lg .middle } __EDOA__

    ---

    Emergency Department Observation Area for short-stay patients.

    - Extended observation (<24 hours)
    - Treatment completion
    - Awaiting investigations or specialist review

</div>

---

## :material-door: Entry Points

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

-   :material-ambulance:{ .lg .middle } __Ambulance Entrance__

    ---

    - Direct access to Resuscitation Area
    - Dedicated bay for ambulance arrivals
    - Emergency access for time-critical patients

-   :material-walk:{ .lg .middle } __Walk-in Entrance__

    ---

    - Main ED entrance for self-presenting patients
    - Triage desk for initial assessment
    - Waiting area for patients and families

</div>

---

## :material-information: Patient Flow

!!! example "Typical Patient Journey"
    
    1. **Arrival** - Ambulance or walk-in
    2. **Triage** - Initial assessment and priority allocation
    3. **Registration** - Administrative details
    4. **Clinical area** - Allocated based on acuity
    5. **Assessment** - Medical review and investigations
    6. **Treatment** - Interventions and management
    7. **Disposition** - Discharge, admission, or transfer
