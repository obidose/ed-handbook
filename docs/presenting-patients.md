# :material-presentation: Presenting Patients

!!! info "Structured Presentation"
    When presenting patients to senior clinicians, it's essential to follow a structured approach. This helps ensure clear communication and demonstrates your clinical reasoning.

!!! success "Most Important Rule"
    **Always have an impression and a plan** - It doesn't matter if it's wrong! Seniors want to see your thinking process and help guide your clinical reasoning.

---

## :material-format-list-checks: Key Principles

!!! success "Effective Patient Presentation"
    - **Structured** - Use a consistent format (e.g., ISBAR)
    - **Complete** - Include all relevant clinical information
    - **Concise** - Focus on key points, avoid unnecessary detail
    - **Clinical reasoning** - Show your thought process
    - **Have a plan** - Even if you're unsure, suggest next steps

---

## :material-chat-processing: ISBAR Framework

!!! example "ISBAR - Structured Communication"
    
    **I - Identify**
    
    - Patient name, age, location
    - Your name and role
    
    **S - Situation**
    
    - Brief summary of presenting complaint
    - Current clinical status
    
    **B - Background**
    
    - Relevant medical history
    - Medications and allergies
    - Key investigation results
    
    **A - Assessment**
    
    - Working diagnosis
    - Clinical impression
    
    **R - Recommendation**
    
    - Outstanding tasks
    - Plan and follow-up required
    - Any concerns or safety issues

---

## :material-clock-alert: When to Handover

- **Shift changeover** - End of your shift
- **Going to break** - Extended breaks during shift
- **Transferring care** - Patient moving to another area/team
- **Escalating concerns** - When seeking senior input
- **Leaving department** - Any time you're leaving the clinical area

---

## :material-alert: Red Flags in Handover

!!! danger "Don't Forget"
    - **Time-critical tasks** - Procedures or reviews due soon
    - **Deteriorating patients** - Any concerning trends
    - **Outstanding results** - Tests pending that may change management
    - **Communication issues** - Family concerns, complex social situations
    - **Safety alerts** - Behavioural concerns, security issues

---

## :material-lightbulb: Best Practice Tips

!!! tip "Presentation Excellence"
    - **Prepare beforehand** - Review notes and results before presenting
    - **Be concise** - Respect the senior's time
    - **State your impression** - Even if uncertain, say what you think
    - **Propose a plan** - Show you've thought about next steps
    - **Ask specific questions** - "What would you do?" vs "I'm thinking X, does that sound reasonable?"
    - **Be ready for teaching** - Seniors may ask why you think something
    - **Take notes** - Write down their recommendations and reasoning
    - **Document the discussion** - Record who you spoke to, when, and the agreed plan

!!! warning "Don't Forget to Document"
    Always document your discussion with the senior in the patient notes. Include who you spoke to, when, and what was agreed.
