## :material-account-voice: History & Examination

### Taking a Good History

!!! example "Structured Approach"
    - **Presenting complaint** - What brought them in?
    - **History of presenting complaint** - Details and timeline
    - **Past medical history** - Relevant conditions
    - **Medications** - Current medications and allergies. Important medications vary by presentation, but always ask specifically about anticoagulants as they can significantly alter management
    - **Social history** - Smoking, alcohol, drugs. Living situation, support, functional status
    - **Systems review** - Relevant systems

!!! tip "Don't Skip the Basics"
    - A thorough history is often more valuable than investigations
    - Let the patient tell their story
    - Ask clarifying questions
    - Document clearly

### Physical Examination

!!! success "Focused but Thorough"
    - **Vital signs** - Don't forget to check them
    - **Focused examination** - Based on presenting complaint
    - **Relevant systems** - Don't just examine the obvious
    - **Safety netting** - Look for red flags

## :material-test-tube: Initial Investigations

### Ordering Investigations

!!! warning "Targeted Investigations"
    - Review any **relevant investigations already done**
    - Order investigations where you are **confident they are needed**
    - **Don't over-investigate** - think about what will change management
    - Every investigation should answer a clinical question

### Common Investigations

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

-   :material-water:{ .lg .middle } __Bloods__

    ---

    - FBC, U&E, LFT, Coag - as appropriate
    - Troponin - chest pain
    - D-dimer - PE/DVT
    - Lipase - abdominal pain
    - TFT - various presentations
    - Lactate - sepsis
    - Blood gases - respiratory/metabolic concerns
    - Blood cultures - if febrile

-   :material-radioactive:{ .lg .middle } __Imaging__

    ---

    - X-rays - fractures, chest pain, SOB
    - CT - head injury, PE, abdominal pain (generally discuss with senior first)
    - Formal ultrasound - often hard to get but may be useful in working hours
    - MRI - infrequently an ED scan (exception: ?cauda equina)

-   :material-sine-wave:{ .lg .middle } __Point of Care Ultrasound__

    ---

    Talk to seniors about POCUS:
    
    - FAST scan
    - AAA assessment
    - Echo
    - Lung ultrasound
    - Vascular access
    - Limited MSK

-   :material-heart-pulse:{ .lg .middle } __Bedside Tests__

    ---

    - ECG - chest pain, palpitations, SOB
    - Urinalysis - abdominal pain, UTI symptoms
    - Blood glucose - altered consciousness
    - Pregnancy test - women of reproductive age

</div>

## :material-lightbulb: Formulating Your Plan

### Initial Impression

!!! example "After Your Assessment"
    - What is the **most likely diagnosis**?
    - What are the **important differentials** to consider?
    - What **can't be missed**?
    - How **unwell** is the patient?

### Creating a Plan

!!! success "Always Have Next Steps"
    Your plan should include:
    
    1. **Investigations needed** - What else do you need to know?
    2. **Treatment started** - What can you start now?
    3. **Likely disposition** - Discharge, admit, or uncertain?
    4. **Time frame** - When will you review?

!!! warning "It's OK to Be Wrong"
    - Your initial plan may change with new information
    - Seniors expect you to have a plan, not necessarily the right one
    - The process of thinking through it is valuable
    - Be ready to adapt
