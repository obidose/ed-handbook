    Discharging Patients

    Safe Discharge Process

    Guidelines for safely discharging patients from the Emergency Department.

    Documentation

    Clinical Coding

    WebPAS Coding Requirements

    Complete the clinical coding on WebPAS including:

    • Diagnosis - Primary presenting complaint and diagnosis
    • Treatments provided - All interventions and medications
    • Procedures performed - Any procedures done
    • Outcome - Discharged home, to care facility, etc.

    Why This Matters

    • Accurate coding ensures correct funding
    • Tracks department activity and outcomes
    • Required for national data collection
    • Medicolegal record

    Clinical Notes

    Complete Documentation

    Your discharge notes should include:

    • History and examination findings
    • Investigation results and interpretation
    • Diagnosis or working diagnosis
    • Treatment provided
    • Discharge plan and safety netting
    • Follow-up arrangements
    • Information given to patient

    GP Communication

    Discharge Summary

    What to Include for Primary Care

    Feed back relevant information to the GP/primary care:

    • Presenting complaint and ED diagnosis
    • Investigations performed and key results
    • Treatment given in ED
    • Medications prescribed - new or changed
    • Follow-up required or recommended
    • Safety netting advice given to patient

    Tone and Language

    Collaborative Approach

    Never tell primary care what to do

    • "The GP must arrange X-ray in 2 weeks"

    • "Suggest repeat X-ray in 2 weeks if symptoms persist"

    • "Start patient on this medication"

    • "Started on medication, may benefit from continuation if symptoms ongoing"

    Suggest and advise - allow primary care to make their own decisions

    Medications Requiring Follow-up

    Handover to GP

    Where medications are likely to need continuing:

    • Note this clearly to the GP
    • Specify the duration of treatment started
    • Indicate if repeat prescription may be needed
    • Explain rationale for medication
    • Include any monitoring requirements

    Medications

    Prescribing New Medications

    Discharge Prescriptions

    Provide any new medications the patient needs:

    • Appropriate quantity for duration of treatment
    • Clear dosing instructions
    • Indication on the label
    • Side effects discussed with patient

    Documentation

    Record Keeping

    • Document medications prescribed
    • Include duration and follow-up instructions
    • Note what patient education was provided
    • Record if patient declined any medications

    Common Discharge Medications

    • Analgesia

      • Appropriate to pain severity
      • Consider escalation ladder
      • Safety netting for side effects

    • Antibiotics

      • Clear duration and indication
      • Red flag symptoms to return
      • Complete the course advice

    • Chronic Medications

      • If new diagnosis requiring long-term treatment
      • Clearly communicate to GP
      • Arrange appropriate follow-up

    Nursing Handover

    Communicate the Plan

    Critical Communication

    Make sure the nurse looking after the patient is aware of the plan:

    • Discharge destination - Home, care facility, etc.
    • Any final tasks needed - Medications, wound care, etc.
    • Medications prescribed - What needs to be dispensed
    • Follow-up arranged - Any appointments made
    • Special instructions - Mobility aids, wound care supplies, etc.

    Team Communication

    Before You Leave

    • Update the nurse before you leave the department
    • Don't just update WebPAS and disappear
    • Check if nurse has any questions
    • Ensure patient is ready for discharge

    Final Safety Checks

    Cannula Check

    Always Check for Cannulas Before Discharge

    ALWAYS check for cannulas before discharging patients!!!

    • Remove unnecessary IV access
    • Document if deliberately left in place (and why)
    • Patient safety issue - retained cannulas cause harm
    • Check BOTH arms
    • Document cannula removal in notes

    Other Device Checks

    Don't Forget

    • Urinary catheters - Should they stay or go?
    • ECG leads and monitors - Remove before discharge
    • Oxygen masks - Not needed if sats normal on air
    • ID bands - Should remain on until patient has left

    Discharge Planning

    Safe Discharge Criteria

    Is It Safe to Go Home?

    Consider:

    • Clinically stable and improving
    • Appropriate investigations completed
    • Treatment initiated and effective
    • Safe environment to go to
    • Social support adequate
    • Patient understands the plan
    • Follow-up arranged where needed

    Safety Netting

    Red Flag Advice

    Always provide clear return instructions:

    • Specific symptoms to watch for
    • When to seek help urgently
    • Who to contact for advice
    • Timeframe for expected recovery
    • Written information where possible

    Follow-up Arrangements

    • Hospital Follow-up

      • Outpatient appointments
      • ED re-check if needed
      • Specialty review
      • Book before discharge where possible

    • GP Follow-up

      • Routine wound checks
      • Repeat prescriptions
      • Ongoing management
      • Clearly communicate expectations to GP

    • Other Services

      • District nursing
      • Physiotherapy
      • Social services
      • Arrange or provide contact details

    Special Circumstances

    Vulnerable Patients

    Extra Considerations

    For elderly, frail, or vulnerable patients:

    • Social assessment - Can they cope at home?
    • Mobility aids - Do they need equipment?
    • Support services - Does help need arranging?
    • Carer availability - Who will look after them?
    • Cognitive ability - Can they follow instructions?

    Paediatric Discharges

    Children and Young People

    • Ensure parent/guardian understands the plan
    • Provide written information suitable for parents
    • Clear return advice for worried parents
    • Consider safeguarding where relevant

    Discharge Checklist

    Before Patient Leaves

    • Clinical coding completed on WebPAS
    • Discharge summary sent to GP
    • Medications prescribed and dispensed
    • Patient given discharge instructions
    • Safety netting advice provided
    • Follow-up arranged where needed
    • Nursing team informed of discharge
    • Cannulas and devices removed
    • Patient able to mobilise safely (or transport arranged)
    • Patient has somewhere safe to go

    Common Pitfalls

    Avoid These Mistakes

    • Discharging before investigation results available
    • Not providing adequate analgesia
    • Forgetting to remove cannulas
    • Not informing the nurse
    • Inadequate safety netting
    • Telling GP what to do rather than suggesting
    • Not documenting discharge discussion
    • Discharging patient who can't cope at home
